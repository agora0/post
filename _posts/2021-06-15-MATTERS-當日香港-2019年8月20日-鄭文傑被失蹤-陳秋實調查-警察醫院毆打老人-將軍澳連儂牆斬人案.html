內地官媒招標Twitter推廣項目曝光：斥資125萬　增加58萬粉絲

撰文：許祺安 2019-08-20 23:08 最後更新日期：2019-08-20 23:08 [香港01]

內地官媒日益重視網絡宣傳，不僅在內地社交媒體，如微博、微信上開設賬號，在境外社交媒體，如Twitter、Facebook等遭內地封鎖的網站上亦開設賬號，「講好中國故事」。

中國政府採購網顯示，日前，中新社以國家財政資金採購Twitter推廣項目，希望藉此增加58萬粉絲，而一家名為「一網互通（北京）科技有限公司」的企業以近125萬元（人民幣，下同）中標。

中國新聞社，英文名為「China News Service」，簡稱「CNS」，是中國內地僅有的兩家通訊社之一，直屬於中共中央統戰部。目前，中新社在Twitter上有兩個賬號，分別為中文賬號「中國新聞社」以及英文賬號「China News 中國新聞網」，前者註冊於2013年，目前有43萬粉絲，後者註冊於2011年，目前有59.2萬粉絲。

中國政府採購網日前在其網站上公佈了《中國新聞社中新社、中新網Twitter帳號推廣政府採購項目中標公告》，並附上了《中國新聞社中新社、中新網Twitter帳號 推廣政府採購項目 招標文件》以及《中標公告》文件。

香港01記者翻閲公告得知，該公告發佈於8月16日，而根據《招標文件》中《採購需求》章節介紹，其需求內容為「Twitter粉絲頁面粉絲增長推廣項目」。《招標文件》指，要在全球範圍內進行推廣，重點是華人較多的國家及關注中國發展的英語國家和地區，而Twitter增粉量要求為58萬，「營銷推廣時間安排為2019年合同簽署日起至2020年2月29日止」，該項目資金來源為財政資金。

《招標文件》還表示，投標人應「針對本項目需求配備經驗豐富的專業優化師，必須具備豐富的海外Twitter平台廣告投放經驗，熟悉海外廣告投放的規律和投放方法」。

而《中標公告》則顯示，「一網互通（北京）科技有限公司」中標，中標金額1248800元，合同履行期為2019年08月16日-2019年09月15日。

據內地徵信網站《天眼查》顯示，一網互通（北京）科技有限公司成立於2017年，註冊資本1162.791萬人民幣。

不過，雖然中新社大力推廣，但社交網站Twitter和Facebook在19日宣佈，關閉多個來自中國內地的帳戶，稱有關帳戶獲官方支持，針對本港近日的示威活動散播不實言論。Twitter還宣佈，將不再接受國家資助的媒體廣告。

中國外交部20日被問及對此事件時表示，不瞭解事件的具體情況，但14億中國人有權利表達自己的觀點和看法。

據紐約時報報道，Twitter已經表示，在禁止中國國有媒體進行推文推廣的禁令生效之前，將給國有媒體一個月的時間離開其廣告平台。

與Twitter不同，Facebook表示不會禁止中國國有媒體的廣告。該公司表示，將「繼續研究我們同國有媒體相關的政策」，並將密切審查在該公司發布的廣告，以便確定它們是否違反了公司政策。據《紐約時報》報道，通過其官方媒體機構，中國政府一直是Facebook廣告的大買家。

港男從深圳過境「被失蹤」　最後簡訊曝光：替我祈禱

2019/08/20 13:24:00 三立新聞台

記者魏文元／綜合報導

在香港情勢動盪之際，一名香港男子日前去了一趟深圳竟然疑似「被失蹤」，家屬心急如焚，這名28歲香港男子是英國駐港領事館的員工，8月8日赴深圳開會，原本預計當天晚間返港，沒想到就此人間蒸發，到現在十多天了音訊全無，傳出已在中國被拘留，英國外交部對此表示高度關注，正協助尋人。

▲Simon至今音訊全無。（圖／翻攝自絲綢之路經濟發展研究中心官網）

照片中戴眼鏡的西裝男名叫Simon，是英國駐香港領事館的員工，但他8月8日到深圳開會，原本預計當天晚上就會搭高鐵返港，沒想到正在過境時卻就此人間蒸發，至今音訊全無。

英國首相強森：「我支持他們（香港示威者），我很樂意替他們發聲，同時支持他們不斷向前挺進。」

中國外交部發言人耿爽：「香港居民的自由是英方爭取來的，這種說法簡直是厚顏無恥。」

中英兩國近來為了香港問題不斷隔空交火，如今英國駐港領事館的員工竟然疑似從深圳返港時「被失蹤」，傳給女友的最後簡訊中只說「正在準備過境」，還加了句「替我祈禱」之後就此失聯。家屬心急如焚報警尋人，中國公安稱沒有抓捕記錄，但向港府追查後得知Simon已在中國被行政拘留，原因和地點不詳。

被逮捕港人：「他們（警方）想知道誰創立了（群組）、背後的目的是什麼。」

其實反送中抗爭爆發以來就傳出有港警駭入通訊軟體監控民眾，失蹤港男在深圳參加活動時也透露，已特地把通訊軟體和記錄全部刪除，是否早有預料可能會被監看，也讓整起失蹤案疑點重重。

如今香港正值多事之秋，英國駐港領事館又有員工去了深圳，回港時被中國扣留，宛如秘密「被送中」，英國外交部也高度關注，並承諾將盡力協助家屬尋人。（江韋儒整理）

Family's Statement on the Disappearance of Simon Cheng

1. Simon Cheng 於2019年8月8日上午從香港前往深圳公幹，晚上從深圳搭乘高鐵回港，於約晚上10時許以WhatsApp 及微信告知女友正在過關，其後失去聯絡。

Simon Cheng travelled from Hong Kong to Shenzhen in the morning of 8 August 2019 for a business trip. He travelled back to Hong Kong by high speed rail on the evening of the same day, and sent messages to his girlfriend via WhatsApp and Wechat while he was about to pass custom at about 10 pm. 2. 家人於 8月9日 曾向警方及入境處求助，僅被告知可自行回內地報案。入境處與駐粵辦聯繫後，於8月10日口頭告知家人Simom被行政拘留，但稱無法得悉拘留原因、場所和期限。

We have sought assistance from the Hong Kong Police and the Immigration Department on 9 August, and were only told that we could travel to mainland China ourselves to report a missing case to the Public Security there. On 10 August, the Immigration Department, after getting in touch with the Hong Kong Economic and Trade Office in Guangdong, told us verbally that Simon has been administratively detained, but said that they could obtain no information on why, where and for how long he was to be detained.

3. 家人至今未有收到內地有關部門的行政拘留通知書；跟據相關法例，該通知書理應於Simon被扣押24小時內發出。家人亦從未收過任何文件確認Simon 是被當局正式拘留。

Till now, we have not received any Notice of Administrative Detention, supposed to be sent out within 24 hours of a person's detention. We simply have never received any documentation confirming that Simon has been formally detained by the authorities.

4. Simon 受僱於英國領事館，家人於8月9日向英國領事館求助，而領事館亦表達高度重視，會嘗試瞭解情況，但一直無法得知更多的資訊。

Simon is an employee of the British Consulate. We have sought assistance from the British Consulate on 9 August. The Consulate expressed their utmost concern and said that they would try to obtain further information, but to date no further information has been obtained.

5. 經律師查證，該案為 深圳市公安局負責，但至今仍未能查知Simon 被關押於何處，亦未能會見律師。深圳市拘留所、寶安區拘留所、南山區拘留所都沒有Simon 的影子。廣洲及深圳的兩家派出所，以及廣州鐵路公安局均稱不知道Simon 的下落。

結果他沒有被打過，但的確感受到示威者的戒心：「我剛跟示威者聊兩句，她就問我是不是公安嗎，我就主動把身份證給她看；離開維園時我在拍影片，旁邊的人馬上說『別拍』、瞪我，解釋他們要保護這裡參與活動的人。」

他說這些事在內地無法看到，那在資訊接收不對等的情況下，兩地的民眾還有可能和解嗎？他覺得要靠對話：「我很多朋友就跟我說，幫我帶一個祝福給他們，不是所有大陸人都恨他們、罵他們。」

親身來到香港，將所見所聞分享到網上，他並不覺得自己特別勇敢。不過，現時他的微博已被禁言，原定一星期的旅程亦因受到各方壓力而中斷，他指已有心理準備將會被吊銷律師執照：「我也覺得自己很衝動，我考律師執照考三年，現在也許我花三天的時間，就要毀掉我過去三年的努力，值得嗎？可能是不值得。我也盡量以最溫和的方式，不違反兩邊任何一邊的法律和道德的情況下，來做這件事情，但是一切成果就自己來承擔。」

Chinese nationalists crash Hong Kong rally in Toronto with luxury cars

blogTO 2019年8月20日

[Please see related videos here]

Rising political tensions between China and the special administrative region of Hong Kong have been spurring enormous protests around the world in recent months, many of them escalating to the point of violence.

A large-scale demonstration in Toronto this weekend was no exception as duelling protest groups screamed, smacked each other and, in the case of Chinese government supporters, blocked off traffic with their luxury supercars.

Thousands of people gathered outside Toronto's Old City Hall on Saturday afternoon for a planned protest in support of Hong Kong, where China's government is said to be cracking down on democratic rights and freedoms.

What started earlier this summer as a series of demonstrations against a controversial extradition bill has turned into what Vox describes as "a broader fight to protect Hong Kong's freedoms and civil liberties against encroachment from the Chinese government."

"Over the past few months, the demonstrations have evolved from millions marching through the streets, to groups of protesters in hard hats storming government headquarters and shutting down the city's international airport for two days," explains CNN.

"Protesters are now demanding greater democracy and an inquiry into alleged police brutality during past demonstrations. Hong Kong's billionaires are calling for order. And as unrest intensifies, Beijing's tone is becoming increasingly heated."

Things were certainly heated at the pro-Hong Kong rally in Toronto on Saturday, especially after counter-protesters arrived to the scene in droves.

Upon arriving to the scene, Chinese nationalists reportedly blocked the supporters of Hong Kong from participating in a planned march around the Eaton Centre.

The pro-democracy group decided to stay put in peaceful protest, according to CTV, as counter-protesters chanted "One China!" and "Loser! Loser!"

Many on Twitter are criticizing the pro-China group in light of footage that shows young people arriving to the protest in very expensive cars.

The cars, all of them waving Mainland China flags, stopped to rev their engines as protesters assembled in support of Hong Kong.

Similar behaviour was observed in Vancouver during one of many pro-democracy rallies on Saturday.

"Today in Toronto, the pro-China groups are attacking the peaceful Hong Kong protests," tweeted one observer. "The Chinese nationalist shown up in fancy cars and call HKers 'poor garbage' also threatens to kill all HKers."

"Pro China counter protest with a flagrant display of wealth?" wrote another. "They clearly are oblivious to how ridiculous they look."

"Mainland Chinese stage counter-protest in Toronto against Hong Kong demonstrators — who are protesting for democracy & rule of law — by driving their 'patriotic ferraris.'" wrote NPR journalist Frank Langfitt in response to the move.

"Were I writing a satirical novel, I would never have had the imagination to invent this scene."

Canada's Minister of Foreign Affairs, Chrystia Freeland issued a statement with her EU counterpart on Saturday addressing tensions between China and Hong Kong.

"For the last two months, large numbers of citizens have been exercising their fundamental right of assembly," it reads. "However, there has recently been a rising number of unacceptable violent incidents, with risks of further violence and instability."

"It is crucial that restraint be exercised, violence rejected and urgent steps taken to de-escalate the situation."

China's government, in turn, has told Freeland to "immediately stop meddling in Hong Kong affairs and China's internal affairs."

Said a spokesperson for the Chinese Embassy in a statement on Sunday: "The Canadian side should be cautious on its words and deeds when it comes to Hong Kong."
